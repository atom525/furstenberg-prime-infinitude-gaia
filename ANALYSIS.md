## Summary

The paper presents an elegant topological proof of the infinitude of primes. The argument defines a topology τ on ℕ generated by coprimality sets σ_n = {m : gcd(n,m) = 1}, proves that prime density in this topology is equivalent to infinitude (Theorem 0.7), then establishes density by showing the closures of prime singletons cover N₁ via the Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic (Theorem 0.9). The reasoning structure is clean and linear: every step is a strict mathematical deduction with no empirical uncertainty. The knowledge graph is a tree of depth 4 with one key fan-out point (thm_base feeds 4 downstream conclusions). The only external inputs are well-established mathematical facts (GCD multiplicativity, FTA, basic topology lemmas).
